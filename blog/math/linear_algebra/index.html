The column space of matrix A, Col(A), is:
• the set of all possible outputs of \( A\vec{v}\)
• the span of \(A \)'s colum vectors (all linear combinations of \(A\)'s colum vectors)
• always has the zero vector



How much the area / volume of the region created by the vectors changes after the transformation

\[ A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \] \[ determinant = ad-bc \] • Det = 0 if transformation reduces dimension for 2x2 to a point/line, for 3x3 to a plane
• Det = 0: the columns of the matrix are linearly dependent
• Use right-hand rule for orientation in 3D
• \( det(M_1 M_2) = det(M_1) det(M_2) \)

Geometrically, the dot product expresses the magnitude and alignment of one vector to another vector.
\( A \dot B = |A| |B| cos \theta \)
• \(\theta = 0^o \): Same direction. Dot product is maximized because \(cos(0^o) = 1\)





\[ A A^{-1} = I \] where \( I \) is the identity matrix such as \[ \mathbf{I}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \] \[ A^{-1} = \frac{1}{|A|} \begin{bmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{bmatrix} \] where \(|A|\) is the determinant of A





The space that gets squished to a lower dimension (space of all vectors that becomes null - lands on the zero vector)

if \(A\) is an m\(\times\)n matrix, then \( N(A) = \{ \vec{x} ∈ ℝ^n | A \vec{x} = 0 \} ; \vec{0} ∈ ℝ^m \)



\( \vec{u}\) and \( \vec{v}\) are orthogonal if \( \vec{u}^T\vec{v} = 0\)



Principal components are the eigenvectors of an m\(\times\)n matrix that contains the samples (n samples, m variables).

1. Create an m\(\times\)n matrix that contains the samples
2. Calculate matrix \(B\) which is an m\(\times\)n matrix whose ith column is \(\vec{x}_i - \vec{\mu}\). \(B = |\vec{x}_1 - \vec{\mu}| ...|\vec{x}_n - \vec{\mu}|\) Standardize the samples so that the mean is zero \(x_i-\mu\)
3. Calculate \(S\), the covariance matrix. \(S = \frac{1}{n-1}BB^T\) symmetric, diagonals are variances
4. Find the eigenvalues and orthogonal eigenvectors   \(\lambda_i\): largest explains the most variation
  \(\vec{u_i}\) are eigenvectors - the principal components



The number of dimensions in the column space of a matrix.



The decomposition of non-square matrices into eigenvectors:

\( A = U \sum V^T \)

where \(U\) is m\(\times\)n and \(V\) is n\(\times\)n

The span of \( \vec{v} \) and \( \vec{w} \) is the set of all their linear combinations (\( a\vec{v} + v\vec{w}\)), where \(a \) and \(b \) can be any real number.

Also known as the column space of (\( a\vec{v} + v\vec{w}\))

Note that a set is all points where the tip of the vector (\( a\vec{v} + v\vec{w}\)) sits.
